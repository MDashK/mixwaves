StepMania Songs

Thanks to our dear fan and friend Sestren NK, now you can play Daytona USA 2 songs in StepMania. For those who don't know, StepMania is mainly a Dance Dance Revolution simulator. You dance the songs in a dance pad, with the purpose of matching the steps.

This simfile/stepfile was designed for use with Stepmania v3.9 and above. To download StepMania, go to http://www.stepmania.com and download the install package. As of this update, v5.0 alpha 1a is the most recent.
The song also works with DWI (Dance With Intensity) but that simulator has been discontinued since 2003. If you wish to download it anyway, go to http://dwi.ddruk.com

Sestren NK says: In the program folder of Stepmania or DWI, go into the songs folder. In it should be a list of album folders. If not, create one and name it whichever you please. Place the ZIP in one of the album folders, and extract it from there. It should create a Skyscraper Sequence folder, and within it the song MP3 file, graphics and DWI file.
This is how it should appear: Stepmania > Songs > (Album Folder) > (Skyscraper Sequence Folder) > Contents (mp3, dwi, graphics)
From there, load up Stepmania or DWI and the song should appear to play. Single steps only, it includes four difficulties. Beginner (1), Light (3), Standard (6), Heavy (8). The steps are designed to be played on a dance pad as opposed to "fingers-only".
If my work on the Techno Fiend theme is anything to go by, the steps should be well synchronized with the music. Enjoy!

A big special thanks to Sestren NK for giving the files and help improve the site. New content to the website and to Daytona USA 2 fans to download is always great.
BTW, funny fact: this all started with the fact that the supposed DDR version of Skyscrapper Sequence that was featured in this page below, and mentioned has a DDR official music, was wrong. This music was never officially featured in DDR, it wasn't even possible since Daytona USA 2 is from SEGA and DDR is from Konami. Infact, this supposed DDR version of the music was created some years ago by Sestren himself, and posted somewhere on the internet, where I eventually came to download also some years ago. When Sesten visited this page and saw the DDR featured version of the music, he came in contact with me and told me about the whole thing. =) Sorry for the wrong info ppl.
